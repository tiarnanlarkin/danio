## The Problem

Fishkeeping is the third most popular pet hobby globally, with 13 million US households keeping freshwater fish. But the mortality rate for aquarium fish — especially among beginners — is staggering.

**Why fish die in home aquariums:**
- Owners don't understand the nitrogen cycle (the #1 cause of fish death)
- Tanks are overstocked because nobody checked compatibility
- Water parameters are never tested
- Maintenance is inconsistent or forgotten
- Beginners rely on pet store advice that's often wrong

**Why this persists:**
- No single, structured resource teaches fishkeeping from scratch
- Information is fragmented across forums, YouTube, and Reddit
- Existing aquarium apps track tanks but don't teach anything
- The learning curve is steep and the consequences (dead fish) are immediate

---

## The Solution

Danio is the first mobile app that combines **fishkeeping education** with **tank management** in a single, gamified experience.

### Core Features

**🎓 Structured Learning**
Interactive, bite-sized lessons covering everything from tank cycling to advanced planted tank care. Each module includes quizzes, visual explainers, and practical tips. Content is science-based and community-reviewed.

**📊 Smart Tank Tracking**
Log water parameters (pH, ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, temperature, GH, KH), track maintenance schedules, and monitor livestock. Trend graphs show progress over time. Smart reminders prevent missed water changes.

**🐟 Species Database**
500+ freshwater species with care requirements, compatibility information, difficulty ratings, and tank size recommendations. Built-in compatibility checker prevents costly (and deadly) stocking mistakes.

**🏆 Gamification**
XP system, daily streaks, achievement badges, and level progression from Novice to Master Aquarist. Streaks reward both learning AND real tank maintenance — creating habits that keep fish alive.

## Developer Story

Hi, I'm Tiarnan Larkin — a solo indie developer from the United Kingdom.

I got into fishkeeping the way most people do: I bought a tank, put fish in it way too early, and watched in horror as they got sick. I spent weeks piecing together information from Reddit threads, contradictory forum posts, and YouTube videos of varying quality. I lost fish. I made expensive mistakes. And I kept thinking: *why isn't there a proper app for this?*

There are apps for learning languages (Duolingo), tracking fitness (MyFitnessPal), managing finances (YNAB) — but nothing that teaches you how to keep a living aquarium healthy. The existing aquarium apps are essentially digital logbooks. They assume you already know what ammonia is, what the nitrogen cycle does, and why you shouldn't put a betta in a bowl.

So I built what I wished existed: an app that teaches you fishkeeping from scratch, tracks your tank, and uses gamification to make the learning stick. I called it Danio — named after the zebrafish (Danio rerio), the most important fish in science and the perfect fish for beginners.

Danio is built with Flutter, designed with care, and backed by real fishkeeping science. It's free because I believe everyone who keeps fish should have access to proper education — not just the people who can afford premium apps or have time to wade through forums.

I'm building this in public, one feature at a time, and I'd love for the fishkeeping community to help shape what comes next.

— Tiarnan

## Interview Topics / FAQ

**Q: Why "Danio"?**
A: The danio (specifically Danio rerio, the zebrafish) is the most important fish in scientific research AND one of the best beginner fish. It's hardy, active, beautiful in schools, and represents exactly what we're about — making fishkeeping accessible and backed by science.

**Q: Why fishkeeping? It's such a niche.**
A: It's the third most popular pet hobby in the world. 13 million US households keep freshwater fish. It's a massive, underserved market with no dominant app. "Niche" is relative.

**Q: How is this different from just watching YouTube?**
A: YouTube is passive and unstructured. You might watch a great cycling video, then get recommended one with terrible advice. Danio gives you a structured learning path with interactive quizzes, progress tracking, and gamification to keep you consistent. It's the difference between watching a language tutorial and actually using Duolingo.

**Q: Why is learning content free?**
A: Because fish die when people don't know how to care for them. Paywalling basic education would be putting revenue ahead of animal welfare. The Pro tier adds convenience features — power users are happy to pay, and their subscriptions fund the free experience.

**Q: What's next for Danio?**
A: Community features (share tanks, discuss species, local fishkeeper networking), AI-powered fish identification, saltwater/reef content modules, and iOS launch. The roadmap is shaped by user feedback.
