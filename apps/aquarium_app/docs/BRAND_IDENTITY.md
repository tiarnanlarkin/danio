## 1. Brand Foundation

### 1.1 Name Analysis: "Danio"

**Verdict: Keep it. It's exceptional.**

"Danio" is a genus of small, hardy freshwater fish — most famously the zebrafish (*Danio rerio*), the single most popular beginner fish on earth. The name derives from the Bangla word *dhani*, meaning **"of the rice field"** — evoking humility, nature, and the shallow waters where these fish thrive in the wild.

**What the name communicates:**
- **Approachability** — Danios are the fish you start with. They're forgiving, colourful, and resilient. Perfect metaphor for a learning app.
- **Insider credibility** — Any fishkeeper recognises the word. It signals "this app was made by someone who actually keeps fish."
- **Scientific grounding** — It's a genus name, lending quiet authority without being pretentious.
- **Memorability** — Two syllables, phonetically clean (DAN-ee-oh), easy to spell, easy to say in any language.
- **Domain/trademark viability** — Short, distinctive, no existing app conflicts in this space.

**What it doesn't communicate (and doesn't need to):**
- It's not self-explanatory to non-fishkeepers — but neither is "Duolingo" (duo + lingo only makes sense in retrospect). The subtitle "Learn Fishkeeping" carries all the explanatory weight.

**Comparison to alternatives considered:**
- "AquaLearn," "FishSchool," etc. — generic, forgettable, sound like 2012 edu-apps
- "Reef," "Tide," "Current" — too broad, trademark nightmares
- "Danio" — niche enough to own, broad enough to grow

### 1.2 Mission Statement

> **Danio exists to make fishkeeping knowledge accessible, engaging, and impossible to forget.**

### 1.3 Brand Promise

**Every fishkeeper — from their first tank to their tenth — can count on Danio to be the most reliable, enjoyable, and science-backed guide to keeping fish alive and thriving.**

In practice this means:
- Content is accurate, current, and never contradictory
- The app respects your time (bite-sized, not bloated)
- Learning feels like play, not homework
- Your data (parameters, livestock, progress) is always safe and useful

### 1.4 Brand Values

| Value | What It Means | How It Shows Up |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Curiosity** | We believe every fishkeeper starts with a question. We feed that instinct. | Lessons are exploratory, not prescriptive. "Why does pH matter?" not "Set your pH to 7.0." |
| **Care** | We care about fish welfare, user wellbeing, and getting the science right. | Content is vetted. Error states are gentle. We never guilt-trip for missed streaks. |
| **Delight** | Learning should spark joy. Maintaining a tank should feel rewarding. | Celebrations, beautiful UI, satisfying animations, surprising micro-interactions. |
| **Honesty** | We tell you what works, not what sells. No pseudoscience. No upselling fear. | "Your ammonia is fine" matters more than "UPGRADE TO SEE IF YOUR FISH WILL DIE." |
| **Progress** | Small, consistent effort beats perfection. Every step forward counts. | XP for every action. Streaks reward consistency. No shaming for being a beginner. |

### 1.5 Brand Personality (Aaker Framework)

Danio's personality sits at the intersection of **Competence** and **Excitement**, with a strong secondary dimension of **Sincerity**.

| Aaker Dimension | Score (1-5) | How It Manifests |
|----------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Sincerity** (honest, wholesome, cheerful) | ★★★★☆ | Warm onboarding, honest copy, genuinely helpful tone. Never snarky or sarcastic. |
| **Excitement** (daring, spirited, imaginative) | ★★★★☆ | Gamification, celebrations, colourful UI, playful mascot potential. "Duolingo energy." |
| **Competence** (reliable, intelligent, successful) | ★★★★★ | Science-backed content, clean data viz, WCAG compliance, professional polish. |
| **Sophistication** (upper class, charming) | ★★☆☆☆ | Not the vibe. This is cozy, not luxury. |
| **Ruggedness** (outdoorsy, tough) | ★☆☆☆☆ | Not applicable. Aquariums are indoor, intimate hobbies. |

**Personality in one sentence:** *Danio is the knowledgeable, encouraging friend who got you into fishkeeping and always knows the answer — but never makes you feel stupid for asking.*

**If Danio were a person:** A marine biology grad student who runs a gorgeous planted tank in their apartment, hosts "fish nerd" nights for friends, explains the nitrogen cycle using beer analogies, and sends you a text when they spot a cool pleco at the fish store.

### 1.6 Positioning Statement

> **For beginner and intermediate aquarium hobbyists who are overwhelmed by contradictory advice and underwhelmed by existing tools, Danio is the gamified fishkeeping app that turns aquarium knowledge into a rewarding daily habit — because it's the only app that actually teaches you fishkeeping, not just tracks it.**

## 2. Visual Identity

### 2.1 Primary Colour Palette

The current teal/amber palette is strong. I'm refining it with specific roles and rationale.

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| **Primary — Deep Teal** | `#3D7068` | Aquatic, calming, trustworthy. Sits between "educational authority" (blue) and "natural/alive" (green). WCAG AA compliant with white text. This is Danio's signature colour. |
| **Secondary — Warm Amber** | `#9F6847` | Warmth, approachability, natural driftwood/sand tones. Prevents the palette from feeling clinical. Balances the cool teal. |
| **Background — Warm Off-White** | `#F5F1EB` | Soft, organic, never sterile. Feels like natural paper or sand. Easier on the eyes than pure white. |
| **Surface — Pure White** | `#FFFFFF` | Cards, modals, elevated surfaces. Creates clear visual hierarchy against the warm background. |

### 2.2 Secondary & Accent Colours

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Accent — Sky Blue** | `#85C7DE` | Water-related UI (parameter charts, tank screens), decorative elements, light CTAs |
| **Accent Alt — Soft Lavender** | `#C5A3FF` | Premium/Pro features, special achievements, "magic" moments |
| **Coral/Peach — Soft Warmth** | `#E8A87C` | Streak flames, warm highlights, secondary CTAs, notification badges |
| **XP Gold** | `#D4A574` | XP counters, level-up moments, achievement badges, reward elements |
| **Success Green** | `#5AAF7A` | Correct answers, healthy parameters, positive states |
| **Warning Amber** | `#C99524` | Caution states, borderline parameters |
| **Error Coral** | `#D96A6A` | Errors, dangerous parameters, wrong answers |

### 2.3 Colour Usage Rules

**The 60-30-10 Rule:**
- **60% — Background/Surface** (`#F5F1EB` / `#FFFFFF`): The canvas. Never compete with content.
- **30% — Primary Teal** (`#3D7068`): Navigation, headers, primary buttons, key UI chrome.
- **10% — Amber + Accents** (`#9F6847`, `#E8A87C`, `#D4A574`): Warmth, rewards, CTAs, personality moments.

**Specific rules:**
1. **Primary teal** is for actions and structure — nav bars, primary buttons, links, selected states.
2. **Amber/coral** is for warmth and reward — never use it for structural UI. It's the "personality" colour.
3. **XP Gold** appears ONLY in gamification contexts — XP counters, badges, level indicators. Never for generic UI.
4. **Lavender** is reserved for Pro/premium features. This creates subconscious association: purple = special.
5. **Semantic colours** (green/amber/red) are ONLY for status — never decorative. Users must learn "green = safe" intuitively.
6. **Dark mode** doesn't invert colours — it shifts backgrounds to deep blue-gray (`#1A2634`) while keeping accent colours luminous. Dark mode should feel like looking into a tank at night.

### 2.4 Typography

**Recommendation: [Nunito](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Nunito) + [Nunito Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Nunito+Sans)**

| Usage | Font | Weight | Why |
|-------|------|--------|-----|
| **Headlines, Titles, Gamification** | Nunito | Bold (700), ExtraBold (800) | Rounded terminals = friendly and approachable. Geometric but warm. Reads as "fun education" not "corporate." Used by Duolingo for similar reasons. |
| **Body Text, UI Labels, Data** | Nunito Sans | Regular (400), SemiBold (600) | Same family, but the sans variant is cleaner for long-form reading and data-dense screens. Maintains family consistency. |
| **Monospace (code/parameters)** | JetBrains Mono | Regular (400) | For water parameter values, technical readouts. Tabular figures ensure alignment. |

**Why Nunito over alternatives:**
- *Inter* — too neutral, feels like a SaaS dashboard
- *Poppins* — overused, reads as "generic startup"
- *Quicksand* — too whimsical, undermines competence
- *Nunito* — warm, rounded, readable, excellent weight range, free, Flutter-friendly via `google_fonts` package

**Type scale (mobile):**
- Display: 28px / Nunito ExtraBold
- H1: 24px / Nunito Bold
- H2: 20px / Nunito Bold
- H3: 18px / Nunito SemiBold
- Body: 16px / Nunito Sans Regular
- Caption: 14px / Nunito Sans Regular
- Overline: 12px / Nunito Sans SemiBold (ALL CAPS, letter-spacing 1.2)

### 2.5 Iconography Style

**Recommendation: Rounded outlined icons — 2px stroke, rounded caps and joins**

**Specific set: [Phosphor Icons](https://phosphoricons.com/) (Regular weight)**

**Why Phosphor:**
- Rounded but not childish — matches Nunito's rounded terminals
- Massive library (6,000+ icons) including many nature/science icons
- Consistent 2px stroke across all icons
- Available as Flutter package (`phosphor_flutter`)
- Duotone variant available for special emphasis (achievements, premium)
- Not overused like Material Icons or Feather

**Icon rules:**
1. **Default state:** Regular weight (outline), primary teal colour
2. **Selected/active state:** Fill variant, primary teal
3. **Disabled state:** Regular weight, `textHint` colour
4. **Achievements/badges:** Duotone variant, with XP Gold as secondary colour
5. **Size:** 24px standard, 20px compact, 32px feature/empty states
6. **Never mix icon families.** Phosphor only, everywhere. Consistency > variety.

### 2.6 Photography & Illustration Direction

**Photography:**
- **Style:** Warm, naturally lit, slightly desaturated (not hyper-saturated Instagram fish photos)
- **Subject:** Real aquariums, real fish, real setups. Never stock photos of people pointing at tablets.
- **Mood:** Cozy, intimate, the feeling of sitting in front of your tank in the evening
- **Treatment:** Slight warm colour grade (shift blacks toward teal, highlights toward warm amber). 8-12px corner radius when placed in UI.
- **Source priority:** User-submitted tank photos (community) > original photography > curated stock

**Illustration:**
- **Style:** Flat with subtle gradients, rounded shapes, warm colour palette. Think "Headspace meets nature documentary."
- **Usage:** Onboarding, empty states, lesson headers, error pages, achievement art
- **Tone:** Friendly, slightly whimsical, never cartoonish. Fish should look like fish, not Nemo.
- **Colour:** Always drawn from the brand palette. Teal + amber dominant, accent colours for variety.

**What to avoid:**
- ❌ Hyper-realistic 3D renders (uncanny, cold)
- ❌ Clip art or stock illustrations (cheap, generic)
- ❌ Neon/saturated fish photography (screams "pet store" not "knowledge app")
- ❌ Human stock photography (faces create demographic assumptions)

### 2.7 Logo & Wordmark Treatment

**Current state:** Water drop icon. Functional but generic — could be a weather app, a hydration tracker, or a plumbing service.

**Recommended direction:**

The Danio logo should be a **stylised danio fish silhouette** — specifically a zebrafish profile, simplified to ~5-6 curves. Think of how Twitter's bird is recognisable in 4 circles, or how Duolingo's owl works at 16×16px.

**Key attributes:**
- **Silhouette-first:** Must work as a single-colour icon at any size (16px to 512px)
- **Swimming forward:** Direction = progress, momentum, learning
- **Slightly playful proportions:** Slightly larger eye, slightly rounded body — friendly without being cartoonish
- **Stripe detail:** One or two subtle stripes (reference the zebrafish's actual pattern) — distinguishes it from a generic fish shape
- **Colour:** Primary teal (`#3D7068`) on light backgrounds, white on dark/teal backgrounds

**Wordmark:**
- "danio" in lowercase Nunito Bold
- Teal colour, tracked slightly (+0.5px letter-spacing)
- The "d" could incorporate a subtle fish tail curve — but only if it doesn't feel forced. Test it; if it's gimmicky, skip it.
- Logo + wordmark should work horizontally (primary) and stacked (square contexts)

**What the logo should feel like:** A knowledge brand that happens to be about fish. Not a pet store. Not a children's game. Not a science journal. The sweet spot between Duolingo's playfulness and Headspace's calm authority.

## 3. Voice & Tone

### 3.1 Brand Voice Attributes

Danio's voice is **Knowledgeable, Encouraging, Conversational, and Playful.**

| Attribute | What It Means | Example | Anti-Example |
|-----------|--------------|---------|--------------|
| **Knowledgeable** | We know our stuff and explain it clearly. No jargon without context. Science-backed but never academic. | "Ammonia spikes usually happen in the first 2-4 weeks. Here's why — and what to do about it." | "Nitrification is the biological oxidation of ammonia to nitrite followed by..." |
| **Encouraging** | We celebrate effort, not just results. Mistakes are learning opportunities, not failures. | "Not quite — but you're thinking about it the right way. The nitrogen cycle takes time to click." | "Wrong! The correct answer was B." |
| **Conversational** | We talk like a friend, not a textbook. Contractions, natural rhythm, occasional humour. | "Your tank's looking good. Nitrates are creeping up though — might be time for a water change." | "Alert: Elevated nitrate levels detected. Recommended action: partial water change." |
| **Playful** | We have fun with fishkeeping. Puns welcome. Never at the user's expense. | "You're on fire! 🔥 Well, not literally. That would be bad for the fish." | "Congratulations on your achievement! We hope you continue to enjoy using Danio." |

### 3.2 Tone Variations by Context

| Context | Tone Dial | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Onboarding** | Warm + excited. Like showing a friend your tank for the first time. | "Welcome to Danio! Whether you just got your first tank or you're a seasoned fishkeeper, we've got something for you. Let's dive in. 🐠" |
| **Lessons** | Clear + engaging. Teacher-friend energy. | "The nitrogen cycle is the single most important thing in fishkeeping. Sounds dramatic? It is. Let's break it down." |
| **Quiz — Correct** | Celebratory + reinforcing. | "Nailed it! 🎉 Ammonia is indeed toxic at any level above 0 ppm. Your fish are lucky to have you." |
| **Quiz — Incorrect** | Encouraging + educational. Never punishing. | "Not quite — but good instinct. pH actually measures acidity, not hardness. GH handles hardness. Easy mix-up!" |
| **Error states** | Honest + helpful. Acknowledge the problem, offer a path forward. | "Something went wrong loading your tank data. Check your connection and try again — your data is safe." |
| **Achievements** | Excited + proud. This is their moment. | "🏆 FIRST WATER TEST! You just did what 90% of new fishkeepers never do. That's huge." |
| **Streak reminders** | Gentle + motivating. Never guilt-tripping. | "Your streak is waiting! A quick lesson keeps the flame alive. 🔥" |
| **Marketing / Store listing** | Confident + benefit-focused. Punchy. | "Think Duolingo, but for fish. Learn fishkeeping through bite-sized lessons, track your tanks, and actually keep your fish alive." |
| **Empty states** | Helpful + optimistic. Show the path. | "No tanks yet? No problem. Add your first tank and Danio will help you keep it thriving." |

### 3.3 Copy Dos and Don'ts

**✅ Do:**
- Use "you" and "your" — it's their journey
- Use contractions (you're, it's, don't, won't)
- Use emoji sparingly — one per message/notification max, never in body text of lessons
- Explain *why*, not just *what* — "Test weekly **because** ammonia can spike without visible signs"
- Keep sentences under 20 words where possible
- Front-load the important information
- Use active voice ("Test your water" not "Water should be tested")

**❌ Don't:**
- Use "we" excessively — this isn't about us, it's about them
- Say "simple" or "easy" — what's easy for you may not be for a beginner
- Use unexplained jargon — if you must use a technical term, define it inline
- Guilt-trip ("You missed your streak!" → "Your streak is waiting!")
- Use corporate language ("leverage," "utilize," "facilitate," "synergy")
- Exclaim everything!!! One exclamation mark maximum per screen.
- Hedge with "just" ("just do a water change" minimises the effort)

### 3.4 Example Rewrites

| Current Copy | Problem | Rewrite |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| "Learn fishkeeping the fun way" | "The fun way" is vague and overused | "Master fishkeeping, one lesson at a time" |
| "Aquarium lessons, tank tracker & fish care guide" | Feature list, not benefit | "Learn fishkeeping. Track your tank. Keep your fish alive." |
| "Log a water test" (button) | Functional but boring | "Log water test 💧" |
| "No data available" (empty chart) | Cold, unhelpful | "No tests yet — log your first one to start tracking trends" |
| "Error loading species" | Generic | "Couldn't load species data. Check your connection and try again." |
| "Achievement unlocked!" | Generic gamification | "🏆 [Achievement Name]! [One sentence about why this matters]" |

## 4. Application Guidelines

### 4.1 App Icon Direction

**Current:** Water drop. **Recommendation: Replace with the danio fish mark.**

The app icon should be the stylised zebrafish silhouette on a teal (`#3D7068`) background with a subtle gradient (slightly lighter at top-left to simulate light from above, like light entering a tank).

**Specifications:**
- Shape: Rounded square (follows platform conventions)
- Background: Teal gradient (`#3D7068` → `#5B9A8B`, top-left to bottom-right)
- Foreground: White fish silhouette, centred, filling ~60% of safe area
- No text in the icon — the fish mark must work alone
- Subtle depth: Thin ambient shadow under the fish (1px, 10% opacity black)

**Why change:** The water drop doesn't differentiate Danio from hundreds of water/weather apps. A fish mark is immediately recognisable, ownable, and builds icon equity over time. When someone sees that fish shape, they should think "Danio."

### 4.2 Feature Graphic Direction (Play Store — 1024×500)

**Layout:**
- Left 60%: Bold headline + tagline on teal background
- Right 40%: Phone mockup showing the Learn screen (lesson path with progress)
- Headline: "**Master Fishkeeping**" in Nunito ExtraBold, white
- Subline: "Bite-sized lessons • Tank tracking • 500+ species" in Nunito Sans, `#E8A87C`
- Decorative: Subtle bubble particles and the fish mark watermarked at 5% opacity

**Key principles:**
- Must be legible at thumbnail size (the headline should be readable at 300px wide)
- Phone mockup shows real UI, not a mockup of a mockup
- Warm, inviting, not "techy"

### 4.3 Screenshot Frame Style

**Frame design:**
- Background: Solid colour from brand palette (alternate between teal, warm off-white, and deep navy)
- Device frame: Minimal, thin bezels (Google Pixel 8 frame for Play Store)
- Caption: Above the device, Nunito Bold, 2 lines max
- Sub-caption: Below the main caption, Nunito Sans Regular, muted colour
- Phone slightly angled (5°) and offset from centre for visual interest
- Subtle shadow beneath device (not a flat layout)

**Sequence colour rotation:**
1. Teal background → white text
2. Off-white background → teal text
3. Deep navy (`#1A2634`) background → white text
4. Repeat pattern

### 4.4 Social Media Visual Direction

**Platform-specific:**

| **Instagram** | Carousel posts, Reels | Teal/amber palette, Nunito headlines, real tank photography with brand colour grade. Carousels: "5 Things Beginners Get Wrong" format. |

**Consistent elements across all platforms:**
- Fish mark watermark (bottom-right corner, 15% opacity) on all branded images
- Nunito font for any text overlays
- Teal + amber colour palette
- Hashtag: `#DanioApp` primary, `#FishkeepingMadeEasy` secondary

### 4.5 In-App UI Implications: Does the Current Design Match the Brand?

**Short answer: 85% yes. The foundation is excellent. Here's what needs attention.**

**✅ What's already on-brand:**
- Teal/amber colour scheme — spot on. Feels aquatic, warm, and premium.
- Dark mode (`#1A2634` deep blue-gray) — beautiful. Feels like looking into a tank at night.
- WCAG AA compliance — shows care and competence.
- Room themes — brilliant feature that adds delight and personalisation.
- Celebration animations (confetti, bubbles) — on-brand playfulness.
- Card-based layout with warm off-white background — cozy, modern, not clinical.

**⚠️ What needs adjustment:**
1. **App icon** — the water drop is the single biggest brand gap. Replace with fish mark.
2. **Typography audit needed** — ensure Nunito/Nunito Sans is used consistently. Check for any system font fallbacks that break the feel.
3. **Empty states** — if any still use generic "No data" messages, rewrite with personality (see Voice section).
4. **Icon consistency** — ensure all icons come from one family (recommend Phosphor). Mixed icon families signal "assembled, not designed."
5. **Onboarding flow** — this is the first-impression moment. Should feel warm and exciting, like unwrapping a gift. If it currently feels like a setup wizard, redesign it.

**🔴 Not yet on-brand (future work):**
- No mascot character yet. Danio would benefit enormously from a simple fish mascot (think Duo the owl) that reacts to user actions. This could be a Rive animation that lives in the Learn tab.
- No custom illustration system. As the brand matures, illustrated lesson headers and achievement art should replace any stock/generated imagery.

## 5. Naming & Tagline

### 5.1 Evaluating "Danio" as a Name

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Memorability** | ✅ Excellent. Short, phonetic, distinctive. |
| **Relevance** | ✅ Directly references fishkeeping (it's a fish genus). |
| **Discoverability** | ⚠️ Searching "danio" returns fish species info, not the app. But "danio app" or "danio fishkeeping" will rank quickly with ASO. |
| **Pronunciation** | ✅ Intuitive in English, Spanish, Portuguese, German. Slightly ambiguous in French (dan-YO vs DAN-ee-oh). |
| **Spelling** | ✅ No common misspellings. |
| **Emotional tone** | ✅ Sounds alive, natural, friendly. Not corporate. |
| **Trademark risk** | ✅ Low — it's a taxonomic term, hard to challenge in the app space. |
| **Scalability** | ✅ Works for freshwater now, could extend to saltwater, ponds, etc. without renaming. |
| **Global appeal** | ✅ Not English-specific. Works across markets. |

**Verdict: "Danio" is a 9/10 name.** The only minor weakness is search disambiguation, and that's solvable through ASO and brand building. Don't change it.

### 5.2 Tagline Options

| # | Tagline | Rationale | Verdict |
|---|---------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **"Master fishkeeping, one lesson at a time"** | Communicates education + bite-sized format. Implies progression. Active and empowering. | **⭐ RECOMMENDED** |
| 2 | **"Your fish will thank you"** | Emotional, outcome-focused, slightly humorous. Creates emotional stakes. | Strong contender for marketing, too casual for store listing subtitle. |
| 3 | **"Learn it. Track it. Love it."** | Rhythmic, covers the three pillars (education, tracking, engagement). Easy to remember. | Good for feature graphics and marketing. A bit generic. |
| 4 | **"Fishkeeping starts here"** | Positions Danio as the starting point for the entire hobby. Bold, declarative. | Strong for awareness campaigns. Slightly exclusionary to experienced keepers. |
| 5 | **"The smartest way to keep fish"** | Positions on intelligence/data. Appeals to the "I want to do this right" crowd. | Good for a more mature audience. Lacks warmth. |
| 6 | **"Level up your fishkeeping"** | Gamification angle. Active, motivating. Implies there's always more to learn. | Works well in-app and on social. Slightly generic "level up" phrasing. |
| 7 | **"Think Duolingo, but for fish"** | Instant comprehension. Leverages Duolingo's brand recognition for positioning. | Excellent for press/PR/pitches. Not suitable as an official tagline (references competitor). |

### 5.3 Current Tagline Assessment: "Learn fishkeeping the fun way"

**Verdict: Replace.**

Problems:
- "The fun way" is a cliché — every gamified app says this
- It centres on the method ("fun") rather than the outcome (mastery, confidence, living fish)
- It's passive — "learn" is generic; it doesn't imply progression or depth
- Doesn't differentiate from a random YouTube playlist

**Primary tagline (store listing, website, hero screens):**
> **"Master fishkeeping, one lesson at a time"**

**Secondary tagline (marketing, social, casual contexts):**
> **"Your fish will thank you"**

**Elevator pitch tagline (press, pitches):**
> **"Think Duolingo, but for fish"**
